# RallyUp Landing Page — Build Plan

## Project Context

This is a **separate project** from the RallyUp mobile/web app. It's a marketing landing page that lives at its own URL.

**Purpose:** A standalone marketing website explaining who RallyUp is, what we do, and why we exist. The QR code on our expo flyer points here so people walking by can quickly understand the mission before (or instead of) trying the live app.

**Where it fits:**
- **Mobile/web app** (separate Vercel project, e.g. `rallyup.vercel.app`) — the actual interactive product
- **Landing page** (this project, separate Vercel project) — the front door / marketing site

---

## About RallyUp

RallyUp is a free, community-driven app that connects underserved NYC youth (ages 8-17) with verified local volunteer sports coaches. We are **finalists at the JA Social Innovation Challenge 2026, presenting at Boston University**.

**Tagline:** "Every Kid Deserves A Coach."

**Mission:** Empower disadvantaged youth by connecting them with volunteer coaches who dedicate their time to making sports accessible — regardless of income or location.

**Team:** Justin Zhong, Asher Cole, Kristina Ma, Amelia Korbas

---

## Audience for This Landing Page

The expo at Boston University is NOT being scored. The actual judging happens later through the slide-deck pitch. The expo audience is:

- University students and faculty browsing
- JA (Junior Achievement) representatives walking around
- General attendees getting a quick gist of various projects

**This means:** Casual browsing audience with 3-5 seconds of attention. The page's job is to *stop them, make them feel something, make them understand quickly.* Not deep evaluation — emotional clarity over information density.

## Aesthetic Direction

### What this site IS:
- **Emotional and human** — real kids, real stakes, real mission
- **Trustworthy and warm** — parents and institutional partners must feel safety
- **Energetic and youth-focused** — sports, community, hope
- **Brand-cohesive with the app** — dark theme + orange `#FF9500` accent so the landing page and app feel like one product

### What this site is NOT:
- **NOT crypto/NFT/space-themed** — no liquid-glass, no neon, no dark cosmic abstraction
- **NOT corporate-stiff** — we're not a SaaS B2B company
- **NOT cluttered with information** — this is awareness/attention, not a whitepaper
- **NOT video-heavy as spectacle** — video serves the mission, not the other way around

### Inspiration we ARE borrowing from sophisticated references:
- Technical polish (smooth scroll, responsive type, careful spacing)
- Liquid-glass UI techniques where they add trust/clarity (sparingly)
- Anton-style strong display fonts paired with clean body text

### Inspiration we are NOT borrowing:
- The cold space-tech aesthetic
- Generic AI-generated abstract video
- Over-the-top hover effects

## Page Structure (sections in order)

### 1. Hero
**The crucial first impression.**

Background: Looping muted video of kids playing soccer (generated by Higgsfield MCP, or sourced from stock footage like Pexels/Coverr if AI generation lands in the uncanny valley).

Overlay content (centered or left-aligned):
- Tagline: **"How will you rally?"** OR **"Welcome to RallyUp"**
- Subtext: *"Every kid deserves a chance to play."*
- Two CTAs:
  - Primary (orange): "Try the App" → opens QR or links to the web app
  - Secondary (outlined): "Become a Coach"

The video should be slightly darkened with an overlay gradient so text remains legible.

A subtle scroll cue at the bottom (animated chevron or "scroll" hint).

### 2. The Problem
Emotional hook drawn from the team's commercial script.

- **Headline:** "1 in 3 kids never have access to athletic mentorship."
- **Sub-copy:** A short paragraph about the gap — paid coaching is inaccessible, YouTube gives no human accountability, kids in underserved communities are left behind.
- Visual: subtle illustration or low-contrast secondary imagery

### 3. How It Works
Two parallel mini-flows. Use cards or a step-by-step horizontal layout.

**For Athletes (3 steps):**
1. Browse coaches by sport, location, and skill level
2. Book a session at a verified safe-zone with parent present
3. Show up, learn, grow — completely free

**For Coaches (3 steps):**
1. Complete the Coach Academy (safety, mentorship, first aid)
2. Get verified through background check
3. Lead sessions, earn community service hours, build a legacy

### 4. Safety First (most important section)
This is our strongest differentiator. Make it the visually heaviest section.

Sub-features with icons and short descriptions:
- **GPS Safe-Zone Check-in** — every session happens at verified DYCD/NYCHA/school partner sites
- **Mandatory Parent Presence** — sessions cannot start until a guardian has GPS-checked-in
- **Background-Checked Coaches** — third-party verified, never private residences
- **Coach Academy** — 4 mandatory training modules before any coach can lead a session
- **Encrypted Messaging** — all communication is E2E encrypted
- **One-Tap Panic Button** — instantly alerts guardians and site administrators

### 5. The RallyUp Difference
Three-column comparison or short pitch:
- **Free forever** — no fees, no subscriptions, no paywalls
- **Volunteer-powered** — real human mentorship, not AI
- **Locally matched** — coaches and athletes from the same NYC neighborhoods

### 6. Vision & Impact
Year-over-year growth visualization (use the data from the organizer):
- Year 1: 125-250 athletes, 15-30 coaches, NYC pilot borough
- Year 5: 10,000-15,000 athletes, 5-8 regional cities

### 7. Meet the Team
Four cards with names and roles:
- Justin Zhong
- Asher Cole
- Kristina Ma
- Amelia Korbas

If photos are available, use them. If not, use clean initial avatars matching the dark theme.

### 8. Footer CTA
- Large QR code linking to the web app
- "Every Kid Deserves A Coach." closing line
- Contact info / social links if available

## Hero Strategy — Critical Execution Rules

The hero is the make-or-break section. Get this right.

### Rule 1: Video as background ONLY
- Higgsfield generates ONLY the soccer footage clip
- Do NOT bake text, transitions, or overlays into the video itself
- All text and transitions are CSS/JS overlays on top of the video

**Why:** Text baked into video can't be edited, isn't responsive, isn't crisp on retina, and isn't accessible. Code overlays are razor-sharp, can be tweaked in seconds, and work on every screen.

### Rule 2: Start clean, enhance later
- Build the basic hero first: looping video + text overlay + CTAs
- Get that deployed and working
- THEN attempt the cinematic "ball kicks into camera → app reveals" transition as a v2 polish

### Rule 3: Don't chase byte-perfect cinematic transitions
- A janky transition reads as amateur — worse than no transition
- A clean fade from soccer footage to your story sections delivers 85% of the emotional payoff for 20% of the effort
- Save the cinematic transition for if you have time to spare

### Rule 4: Performance matters
- Video file should be optimized (compress with HandBrake, keep under 3MB for hero loop)
- Use `<video poster="">` to show a still image while video loads
- Lazy-load video on slow connections
- Always include `autoplay muted loop playsinline` attributes

## Higgsfield Video Generation Notes

When generating the soccer clip:
- **Prompt direction:** Authentic, warm footage of kids (ages 8-17) playing soccer outdoors in an urban setting. Late afternoon golden-hour light. NYC park aesthetic. Joyful, energetic, real.
- **Avoid:** Uncanny-valley faces, overly polished commercial-stock look, generic AI shimmer
- **Test multiple generations** — pick the most natural-looking one
- **Backup plan:** If AI-generated kids look weird or off, fall back to authentic stock footage from Pexels (search "kids soccer", "youth sports"). Free, no licensing issues.

## What "Done" Looks Like for v1

Before declaring the landing page demo-ready, all of these must be true:

- [ ] Loads cleanly on iOS Safari, Android Chrome, desktop browsers
- [ ] Hero video plays automatically on all platforms (autoplay muted loop)
- [ ] Text is crisp and legible on the video at all screen sizes
- [ ] All sections render correctly on mobile (most expo visitors will scan on phones)
- [ ] CTAs work and point to the right URLs
- [ ] QR code in footer scans correctly and links to the app
- [ ] Page loads in under 3 seconds on a typical mobile connection
- [ ] No console errors
- [ ] Looks visually cohesive with the app (same colors, similar vibe)

## What to AVOID

- Don't replicate the NFT/space-themed liquid-glass aesthetic from any reference. It's beautiful craft but wrong audience.
- Don't bake text into video files
- Don't use script/cursive fonts (reads as crypto)
- Don't use neon green (`#6FFF00` or similar) — clashes with our brand
- Don't use AI-generated images of children if they look uncanny — stock footage is safer
- Don't add features beyond what's in this plan without a clear reason
- Don't deploy to Vercel until v1 is tested locally and works
- Don't push the `.env` file or any API keys to GitHub
